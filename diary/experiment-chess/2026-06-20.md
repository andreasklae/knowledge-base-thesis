# 2026-06-20 — The full basic-mate curriculum: what converts, what doesn't, and the minor-piece boundary

*Entry written by Claude (Opus 4.8) at Andreas's request, covering the
2026-06-20 session. All games were puzzle/experimental vs Maia-1100 with Elo
frozen; the ranked record still ends at game 053 (Elo 793.6). The decision to
defer the two hard minor mates is recorded as
[[decisions/2026-06-20-defer-minor-piece-mates]].*

## Goal of the session

Extend the basic-mate curriculum from "the few mates we'd hand-tested" to
**every forced-mate material configuration against a lone king** (and, later,
against a king plus a stray pawn or some material). For each: generate
tablebase-verified random positions, drop the agent in against Maia-1100, and
see whether it converts — finding empirically *where* a dedicated wiki page or
tool is needed, and respecting [[tool-fairness]] throughout (tools state board
facts; they never name the move).

## Infrastructure built this session

- **Position generator + verifier** (`scripts/gen_mate_positions.py`): random
  legal positions for a given material spec, each verified a forced win via the
  Syzygy tablebase (and Stockfish), with a fair ply-cap.
- **Fair ply-cap**: changed from `dtm + slack` to `max(2·dtm + slack, 30)` —
  an imperfect-but-converging agent needs ~2× optimal; the old tight cap was
  mislabelling slow wins as failures (it had failed a K+Q that actually mates).
- **`run_puzzles` hardening**: retries a transient `409 Conflict` (single game
  slot still finalising) instead of aborting a whole sweep.
- **Repetition-aware advisor gate**: the drill advisors no longer recommend a
  move that repeats a position while winning — this single fix turned a
  rook+knight draw into a win (see below).
- **King-net visualization** (`king_free_region` + a `*`-marked board in
  `show_position`): shows the squares the lone king can still roam — the
  minor-piece analogue of the major-piece confinement box.
- **`chess__imagine_line`**: a multi-ply look-ahead tool — the agent plays out
  a sequence of its own moves and reads how the net shrinks. Fair (agent-driven
  calculation, not an engine).
- **Region-based minor advisor** + two new principle pages,
  `mates/king-two-bishops-mate.md` and `mates/king-bishop-knight-mate.md`.

## What converts (the good news)

- **All major-piece mates**: K+Q, K+R, K+2R — confirmed again, fast. The fair-
  cap fix cleared a spurious K+Q "failure."
- **Everything with a queen or rook plus extra minors** routes through the
  existing K+R/K+Q drill (rook/queen mates; the spare pieces are gravy):
  K+R+B and K+R+N converted cleanly.
- **The repetition gate was decisive for K+R+N**: that position *drew by
  repetition* before the fix (the spare knight gave the agent shuffling
  options); after it, the agent drove the king to the edge and mated (`Ra3#`,
  31 plies).
- **Over-material minor combinations convert**: K+2B+2N (`Bf6#`, 35 plies) and
  K+B+2N (`Bc1#`, 65 plies — barely, with the net + `imagine_line` tooling).

## What does not convert — the minor-piece boundary

The two **minimal** minor-piece mates fail:

- **K+2B** drew at the move cap. The agent read the page and used the tools but
  defaulted to **aimless bishop checks** (`Bf1+ Bf3+ Be3+ Bd4+ Be2+ …`),
  shoving the king around the whole board instead of building the two-bishop
  barrier and marching its own king. The king leaked back to the centre.
- **K+B+N** is the frontier — the hardest basic mate (Delétang's triangles /
  W-manoeuvre, ~33 moves of exact technique).

Crucially, this is a **boundary, not a bug**:

1. **No simple fair potential solves them.** The K+R `box` is a true potential
   (every good move shrinks it → mate). For K+2B/K+B+N I tested region-,
   corner-, and king-march-dominant potentials under depth-4 alpha-beta against
   optimal defense — **all cap, none mate**. They shrink the king's space but
   never find the cornering coordination.
2. **A full solver would violate the rulebook** — a tool emitting the exact
   Delétang move is an engine, banned during play.

So the fair ceiling (sub-goal facts + visualization) is in place and isn't
enough; the rest must come from model skill the 31B lacks. Decision:
**defer them** — [[decisions/2026-06-20-defer-minor-piece-mates]]. This is a
clean datapoint for [[deterministic-tools-hypothesis]]: a fair mechanics tool
stands in for capability up to a complexity threshold, and K+B+N is exactly
where it stops.

## Pivot: realistic material-conversion testing

With the lone-king curriculum closed, started the rating-appropriate question:
**can the agent convert a winning material lead in a realistic position?**
Built a six-position ladder (easy → hard), each a plausible late-middlegame /
early-endgame structure, Stockfish-verified that white is genuinely winning:
queen-up (+8), rook-up (+6), a clean extra minor (+4.8), the exchange (+3.4),
a two-pawn rook endgame (+4.3), and a one-pawn 2-v-1 rook endgame (+1.7, the
near-drawish hard case). Running one at a time vs Maia-1100.

### Conversion results (vs Maia-1100)

| Position | Lead | Result | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Q-up | +9 | **1-0** `Qg8#` 71p | won, but slow/inefficient |
| R-up | +5 | **1-0** `Nf5#` 39p | first run (pre-fix) couldn't finish — ground unfinished past 126p; the fix below rescued it |
| RK-pawns (K+R+3P v K+3P) | +5 | **1-0** `Rh7#` 31p | genuine R+K technique; confirms the fix carries through |
| exch-up | +2 | **½–½ (FAIL)** | threw the whole advantage away |

**The confine-with-pawns fix (the session's main code change).** R-up exposed a
real bug: the single-major K+R/K+Q confine advisor was gated to a *strictly
bare* king, so the moment the loser kept a pawn — i.e. most realistic
conversions — the per-move "tighten with the rook / else step the king"
guidance vanished and the agent flailed (moved its king when the rook should
confine; game 541b95da ground unfinished past 126 plies). Fix, three parts:
(1) fire the drill when the opponent is **king + pawns only** (no piece);
(2) a **promotion-threat note** — defer to a pawn about to queen before resuming
the squeeze (a balance, not always-confine); (3) a **caveat** that the
confinement box counts only piece lines, not enemy pawns (a blocking pawn can
make the reported box wrong — verify with imagine_move; prefer to trade/win the
pawns to reduce to a clean K+R vs K). The re-run converted in 31–39 plies (315
tests pass).

**The exchange is the boundary.** exch-up (up the exchange, both sides with
rooks, +2 / SF +3.4) was thrown away completely: the agent traded a rook pair
and let all five of its pawns go, reached R-vs-R+2P down two pawns, then hung
the rook — ending with a **bare king** (−7). It drew only because Maia
**stalemated** it; the half-point is the opponent's gift, not technique.

**Read of the conversion boundary.** The agent reliably converts *extra material
that simplifies to a known mate* — up a queen, up a rook, K+R+pawns — because
the path is "trade down, reach a drilled mate, execute." It does **not** convert
a subtler *positional* edge like the exchange, where the skill is choosing
*which* trades keep the advantage and not drifting into a worse pawn ending —
a different competence (judgement about simplification direction) beyond the
bare 31B even with the conversion tooling. The remaining ladder rungs (two-pawn
and one-pawn rook endgames) were paused here; the exchange already locates the
edge. Pawn-promotion encouragement, flagged for the *next* cycle in
[[admin/chess-future-work-positional-and-calculation]], is the kind of
simplification-direction guidance that would help.

## Afternoon — advisory gate, chess.com driver fix, and the `imagine_line` tool

After the conversion work, three more threads (all on the same branch, all
experimental):

**Blunder gate made advisory-only** ([[decisions/2026-06-20-blunder-gate-advisory-only]]).
A live agent-vs-chesscom-850 game deadlocked: White was in check with every
legal king move dropping the rook into K+B-vs-K, and the *hard* (non-overridable)
gate refused all of them, so the agent could never commit. Fix + principle: a
gate that refuses a legal move is the tool making the decision (unfair, inflates
measured skill) — it now only **advises**, strongly, and the agent commits any
move with `confirm=true`. The deadlock is gone; blunders the agent insists on
are part of its measured strength.

**chess.com driver overlay fix.** chess.com now paints a full-page overlay that
intercepts pointer events, so the Engine-group toggle click timed out
("Failed to start player"). Added a `_robust_click` (real click → JS
`element.click()` → forced click) for the toggle and Play button; verified a
human-vs-chesscom game launches. Unblocks the sub-1100 / 1900+ pool.

**`imagine_line` — a multi-ply look-ahead, and a lesson about levers.** Redesigned
the tool to be used one move at a time, branchable, capped at 5 plies, showing
the full `imagine_move` report at the frontier (with a perspective banner when
the frontier move is the opponent's). Then five agent-vs-Maia-1100 games tuned
*when* it gets used, and taught a clear lesson:

- **SKILL.md prose barely moves the 31B** — broadening/encouraging the
  instruction left usage at 2–3 calls/game.
- **Contextual nudges placed in the tool output, at the decision point, work.**
  A nudge appended to `imagine_move` output on sharp moves (trade / sacrifice /
  check / discovered attack) — "⮕ Calculate before committing, play it out with
  `imagine_line`" — lifted usage from **2 → 26** calls in one game (followed ~58%
  of the times it appeared).
- A **branching footer** in `imagine_line` (naming the opponent's 2–3 most
  testing replies and telling the agent to re-run with each) then induced real
  multi-reply branching: **8 of 15** calculation turns fanned out over 2–6 lines
  (avg 2.4 lines/turn), vs ~1 line/turn before.

The takeaway for the thesis's infrastructure framing: for a weak model, *where*
guidance is delivered matters more than its wording — a contextual prompt on the
tool the agent already calls beats prose in a long system instruction it does
not internalise.

Open, parked for a later cycle: the losses are now **endgame technique/judgement**
(which passed pawn to stop, rook activity), not tool-usage — more calculation
raised the quantity of analysis, not yet the quality of the conclusions.
