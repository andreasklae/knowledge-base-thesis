# 2026-06-10 — Mating patterns & strategy: wiki ingestion, structured memory, mate radar, puzzle mode

*Entry written by Claude (Fable 5) during the autonomous work session Andreas
delegated on this branch; see [[diary/meta/2026-06-10]] for the meta-level
reflection on that delegation. Each section below is one revertible unit
(one or two commits), so reactions can be appended per section.*

## Why this branch exists

The five ranked games before this session told one story: the agent reaches
trivially won positions and cannot finish. Three 150-ply draws with Q+pawns
v Q, R+2B v bare king, and Q+P v R. The diagnosis from replaying the agent
logs: no mating technique (it has never "read" any), no plan persistence
(the turn memory was a single note, overwritten every move — the agent
re-derived "promote the a-pawn" turn after turn while shuffling checks),
and no awareness that a move cap even exists.

## 1. Capablanca ingestion (`f2005cf`)

Converted *Chess Fundamentals* (Gutenberg #33870, public domain) into
`references/raw/chess-fundamentals-capablanca.md`: Ch. I §1–4 + Ch. II
§9–13, every diagram reconstructed to a FEN (read from the Gutenberg
images), every line translated from descriptive notation to SAN and
replayed in python-chess. Two notes: Example 6's diagram was ambiguous in
the image, so the start squares were brute-forced against line legality;
Example 16's printed "5...R-B1" only mates if read as 5...Rc8 (QB1) —
plausibly a 105-year-old notational subtlety nobody machine-checked before.
The opposition position (Ex. 27) was cross-checked against the lichess
tablebase: "whoever moves first wins" holds exactly.

## 2. Nine wiki pages (`06829aa`)

Mating patterns: ladder-mate (the "first mate to look for"), king-queen-mate,
king-rook-mate. Tactics/principles: deflection, luft (both fixed dead links
from back-rank-mate), avoid-stalemate. Strategy: convert-advantage (the
staircase: trade pieces → passed pawn → promote → book mate; "give every
check a job"), make-a-plan. Endgames: king-pawn-endings. All FENs verified;
one bug caught pre-commit (the luft example originally gave the escape
square to the wrong king). Selection bias is deliberate: simple forced wins
over clever ones, per the tutor guidance that promoting a pawn beats hunting
a bishop-and-knight mate.

## 3. Mate & draw radar (`165d98e`)

`show_position` now ends with mechanical facts + wiki pointers: "Opponent
has a bare king; K+R vs K is a forced mate — read king-rook-mate.md",
enemy-king edge/corner + legal-move count, back-rank geometry, passed pawns
with distance, repetition/50-move/move-cap warnings. Everything is material
counting, geometry, or the rules of chess — mechanics-tool territory under
[[2026-06-02-tool-fairness-rulebook]] (which explicitly lists back-rank
vulnerability detection as fair). Replaying the radar over the drawn games
produces exactly the missing nudges ("about 5 of your moves remain").

## 4. Structured turn memory + a real bug (`1b4ae75`, ADR [[2026-06-10-structured-turn-memory]])

`make_move` gains an optional `plan` argument: a standing plan that
persists across turns until replaced (notes still replace each turn;
everything else is forgotten — aggressive forgetting is a feature for a
31B reader). The injection now fires once per attempt and re-renders the
memory in the agent's own voice.

The bug: while reworking the injection I probed pydantic-ai 1.99 with a
FunctionModel and found the old history replacement **dropped the system
prompt from every turn after the first** — since 2026-05-26 the model has
played turns 2+ with no skill list and no harness instructions. Fixed by
re-injecting a SystemPromptPart; regression-tested. Confound note: any ELO
movement after this PR partly reflects this fix, not just the wiki/memory
work.

## 5. Puzzle mode (`202e349`)

`POST /api/games` accepts `initial_fen`; `scripts/run_puzzles.py` drives a
six-position suite (mate-in-1, K+Q, K+R, two-rook ladder, Capablanca's
pawn escort, and the literal final position of drawn game `72aa5cfc`).
Puzzle games land in experimental.csv, ELO frozen — measurement, not
calibration.

## 6. Test sessions (eX3, Gemma 4 31B, Maia-1100 as black)

Three rounds, because the first round found bugs (that is what the puzzle
mode is for):

**Round 1** exposed that puzzle games were *blind*: `show_position` crashed
from turn 3 on (history replay assumed the standard start; python-chess
raises AssertionError, not ValueError, on an illegal push). Only
`backrank-m1` survived it — **1-0, Ra8# in one move**, radar-guided, 4 tool
calls. Fixed in `37e91f6` together with tunnel-blip retries and an
`aborted_reason` field on GameState.

**Round 2** (perception working): `kq-basic` **1-0, Qg7# in 25 plies** —
the configuration's historical failure mode (Q-up positions drawn) now
converts; the standing plan ("shrinking box, bring king up, mate") was
written on move 1 and carried verbatim to the mate. The rest still drew:
kr-basic (50-move rule after 99 plies of rook-checking without opposition
technique), ladder + r2b-convert (threefold — shuffling between two
positions straight past the radar's position-level warning), promote
(escorted the pawn perfectly, then pushed e7+ — the exact
pawn-on-7th-with-check anti-pattern its own wiki warns about — and
abandoned it).

**Round 3** (after `eb5c7c6` put draw flags on candidate moves in
`list_legal_moves`/`imagine_move`): ladder improved visibly — kept both
rooks (round 2 hung them to the enemy king), drove the king to the 8th rank
with a real two-rook net (Ra7+/Rb7) — but couldn't find the final
slide-far-then-mate and drew by threefold at ply 30. r2b ended 0-1 by
resignation: Gemma degenerated into emitting empty `<|channel>thought`
blocks for ten straight attempts without calling a tool — a model-level
failure mode worth tracking, recorded in the game log.

**Net read:** mate-in-1 recognition and K+Q conversion are now solid —
those cover the most common "won but drawn" cases in the ranked data.
Two-rook and K+R *technique* (opposition, waiting moves, slide-away) and
plan *discipline* under repetition warnings remain beyond the bare model
even with pages + flags; that boundary is itself a Phase-1 result. Wiki
pages stay `status: draft` until ranked games confirm them.
